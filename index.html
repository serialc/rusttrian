Instructions

The situation. You and a friend, that is located at some distance from yourself, observe a feature or sound, such as a supply-drop parachuting down or a particular sound. It's quite far and will likely either stop being visible or audible before you reach it. In the examples above this means the air drop will land in the forest or the sound will stop before your reach it. You're likely to underestimate it's distance and landing location due to this. By triangulating the location with your friend's help however, you'll be able to get a good estimate of the distance and location of the feauture.

Using a map with grid cells available, as shown adjacent, note the location (e.g., C1, E5) of each observer. Then for each observer indicate the compass direction of the feature or sound you wish to locate (e.g., North is 0, East 90, South 180, West 270). Click Triangulate and a rough area will be shown.

Due to the innacuracy of your location (the whole grid cell) the search area above, in green, is quite large. You can provide your location more accurately using the precision options that appear after submitting your first four parameters (as shown to the left).

The results provide a much smaller area than before with the added location accuracy. Note that for this improved accuracy search a small uncertainty is added to the directions to provide an area rather than a point as reading compass headings have some error.
